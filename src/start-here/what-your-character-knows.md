# What Your Character Knows

This page summarizes what most characters might know about Emberwreath. It does not explain forbidden ruins or settle mysteries that remain uncertain in play.

## Common knowledge

Most people know Emberwreath is interconnected but not unified. No single crown, temple, guild, species, or island rules the whole archipelago.

Most people know the nine playable species have homeland islands:

- Humans from Breadbasket
- Zivari from Saffra
- Veyr from Sunspire
- Ferren from Greenwood
- Aumari from Tidefall Atolls
- Axali from Hollowdeep
- Scolen from Caelmere
- Kelden from Ashspine
- Thryss from Veilgarden

Most people know trade matters. Food, water, timber, routes, fiber, medicine, pilots, and ports can decide whether communities thrive, struggle, or fail.

Most people know the major eruption 60 years ago changed the archipelago. It still matters to elders, families, ports, institutions, and the way people talk about risk.

Most people know old island traditions still exist, even in places shaped by trade and travel.

## Local knowledge

Local knowledge depends on where your character grew up, worked, sailed, studied, worshiped, or survived. A fisher, porter, pilot, healer, caravan guard, temple attendant, or dock worker may know practical details that outsiders miss.

Your character may know things like:

- Which routes are safe in a certain season.
- Which ports are reliable for water, repairs, medicine, or shelter.
- Which old customs matter on a particular island.
- Which reefs, ruins, or crossings locals avoid discussing too casually.

Ask your GM how much local detail makes sense for your character's origin.

## Rumors and warnings

Most people have heard warnings or rumors about the Caldera, forbidden ruins, taboo reefs, and failed expeditions. These stories are often incomplete, contradictory, or shaped by local tradition.

Most people have also heard of the Veiled Reefs as a newer multi-species pilgrimage tradition tied to sacred waters, dangerous routes, wreck memory, and boundaries. What exactly someone believes about the reefs depends on their home, faith, work, and experience at sea.

Rumors are useful for tone and character choices, but they are not proof.
