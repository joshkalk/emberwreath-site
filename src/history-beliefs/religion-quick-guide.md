# Religion Quick Guide

A character can be devout, casual, skeptical, shaped by mixed traditions, or shaped by a tradition without being personally religious. Old island traditions are not gone. The Veiled Reefs are newer and multi-species. Religion in Emberwreath often concerns survival, obligation, boundaries, memory, restraint, and the consequences of ignoring warnings.


<div class="card-grid">
  <section class="card">
    <h2>Civic-Harvest Covenant</h2>
    <p><strong>Humans, Breadbasket.</strong></p>
    <p><strong>Core idea:</strong> Food security is a public covenant.</p>
    <p><strong>What it sanctifies:</strong> Food, roads, granaries, fair measures, public obligation, and the duty to keep food moving.</p>
    <p><strong>What it warns against:</strong> Hoarding in need, falsifying weights, blocking roads, and letting food spoil through negligence.</p>
    <p><strong>Character use:</strong> You might treat logistics, honest trade, and feeding strangers as sacred duties.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Twin Law of Ember and Stone</h2>
    <p><strong>Kelden, Ashspine.</strong></p>
    <p><strong>Core idea:</strong> Life depends on balance between force and shelter.</p>
    <p><strong>What it sanctifies:</strong> Heat, change, hunger, revelation, shelter, endurance, boundary, and memory.</p>
    <p><strong>What it warns against:</strong> Greed, ignoring volcanic warning, and taking after the land has warned against it.</p>
    <p><strong>Character use:</strong> You might honor warnings, respect limits, and see hunger for more as both useful and dangerous.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Measured Flow</h2>
    <p><strong>Scolen, Caelmere.</strong></p>
    <p><strong>Core idea:</strong> Water becomes life-giving when properly guided.</p>
    <p><strong>What it sanctifies:</strong> Sacred maintenance, canals, reservoirs, locks, measuring, and public water duty.</p>
    <p><strong>What it warns against:</strong> Reckless alteration, hidden damage, poisoning water, and misdirecting water.</p>
    <p><strong>Character use:</strong> You might value maintenance, records, and careful intervention over dramatic fixes.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Wardens of Root and Name</h2>
    <p><strong>Ferren, Greenwood.</strong></p>
    <p><strong>Core idea:</strong> Forest wardship binds the dead, living, and unborn.</p>
    <p><strong>What it sanctifies:</strong> Named groves, hunting paths, root-sites, ancestors, and continuity.</p>
    <p><strong>What it warns against:</strong> Wasteful cutting, burning old groves, hunting for display, and forgetting the dead.</p>
    <p><strong>Character use:</strong> You might carry names, protect old places, or treat memory as a living obligation.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Descent of Held Water</h2>
    <p><strong>Axali, Hollowdeep.</strong></p>
    <p><strong>Core idea:</strong> Hidden reserves are witnessed and owed respect.</p>
    <p><strong>What it sanctifies:</strong> Hidden water, wells, aquifers, sinkholes, underground storage, and ancestor witnesses beneath.</p>
    <p><strong>What it warns against:</strong> Contaminating wells, wasting stored water, false drought accounting, and abandoning lower chambers without rite.</p>
    <p><strong>Character use:</strong> You might keep careful stores, distrust waste, and treat unseen foundations as morally important.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Discipline of Bloom</h2>
    <p><strong>Zivari, Saffra.</strong></p>
    <p><strong>Core idea:</strong> Cultivation is moral discipline.</p>
    <p><strong>What it sanctifies:</strong> Beauty, medicine, dye, spice, fruit, terraces, pruning, patience, and inherited craft.</p>
    <p><strong>What it warns against:</strong> Overharvesting, false medicines, ruined terraces, and treating beauty as mere luxury.</p>
    <p><strong>Character use:</strong> You might see patience, skill, and beautiful work as evidence of responsibility.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Listening Tide</h2>
    <p><strong>Aumari, Tidefall Atolls.</strong></p>
    <p><strong>Core idea:</strong> Survival requires negotiated coexistence with changing waters.</p>
    <p><strong>What it sanctifies:</strong> Lagoons, channels, tide pools, pearl beds, freshwater lenses, and storm signs listened to correctly.</p>
    <p><strong>What it warns against:</strong> Fouling freshwater lenses, pearl theft, and ignoring closed tides.</p>
    <p><strong>Character use:</strong> You might read conditions before acting and treat restraint as practical wisdom.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Hidden Continuity</h2>
    <p><strong>Thryss, Veilgarden.</strong></p>
    <p><strong>Core idea:</strong> Some things survive because they are concealed.</p>
    <p><strong>What it sanctifies:</strong> Fog, hidden valleys, rare herbs, protected routes, memory, and fragile continuities.</p>
    <p><strong>What it warns against:</strong> Reckless revelation, exposing protected places, and turning guarded knowledge into gossip.</p>
    <p><strong>Character use:</strong> You might believe secrecy can be care rather than selfishness.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Mercy of the Boundary</h2>
    <p><strong>Veyr, Sunspire.</strong></p>
    <p><strong>Core idea:</strong> Mercy can mean warning others away.</p>
    <p><strong>What it sanctifies:</strong> Ruin-boundary mercy, route guidance, warning markers, reservoirs, herding roads, and restraint.</p>
    <p><strong>What it warns against:</strong> Entering forbidden ruins for glory, profit, or proof.</p>
    <p><strong>Character use:</strong> You might guide people around danger and refuse dares that mistake boundaries for weakness.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Pilgrimage of the Unreturned Path</h2>
    <p><strong>Multi-species, Veiled Reefs.</strong></p>
    <p><strong>Core idea:</strong> Dangerous waters can make turning back, silence, or safe return sacred.</p>
    <p><strong>What it sanctifies:</strong> Hidden passages, thresholds, drowned memory, taboo lagoons, wrecks, sacred silence, and journeys where turning back matters.</p>
    <p><strong>What it warns against:</strong> Claiming the reefs, mapping certain channels, salvaging taboo wrecks, diving in closed blue holes, and commercializing sacred routes.</p>
    <p><strong>Character use:</strong> You might be a pilgrim, survivor, reef guide, refugee, sailor, skeptic, or someone who respects routes no one should boast about.</p>
  </section>
</div>

The Veiled Reefs are not a blended super-religion and are not owned by one species. They emerged after contact among sailors, wreck survivors, pilgrims, reef guides, refugees, and people whose old island traditions could not fully explain what happened in dangerous waters between homes.
