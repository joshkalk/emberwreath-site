# Timeline

A fast chronology for character creation and table reference.

<div class="card-grid">
  <section class="card">
    <h2>Early homeland era</h2>
    <p>The peoples of Emberwreath mostly live apart on homeland islands, developing local traditions shaped by survival, memory, and environment.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>About 500 years ago</h2>
    <p>Scattered crossings, storms, trade, rescue, curiosity, and survival bring the first sustained contacts between islands and peoples.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Trade age</h2>
    <p>Crossings become routes. Routes become ports, laws, obligations, shrines, and shared systems. Emberwreath becomes connected, but not unified.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Religious change</h2>
    <p>Old homeland traditions remain and often sharpen. New multi-species beliefs appear around dangerous waters, travel, rescue, wrecks, and safe return.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>About 60 years ago</h2>
    <p>A major Caldera eruption darkens seas, damages routes, strains crops and water systems, moves refugees, and reveals how dependent the islands are on one another.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Recovery era</h2>
    <p>Food, water, timber, shipping, medicine, and route systems become politically and spiritually charged as communities rebuild and renegotiate obligations.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Present day</h2>
    <p>Emberwreath functions again, but remains fragile. Most people were born after the eruption; many elders remember it.</p>
  </section>
</div>
