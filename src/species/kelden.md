# Kelden

**Homeland:** Ashspine

The lower path is gone. Tie the child to my harness, step when I say step, and do not look for a road.

## Strength on Unsteady Ground

The Kelden are heavy, basalt-bodied volcanic people from Ashspine, an island of cliffs, ash slopes, sulfur vents, broken ridges, hardy ledge gardens, and settlements built where a bad step can kill. Their bodies are broad, dense, clawed, and mineralized, shaped by ground that shifts, cracks, burns, and falls away.

Kelden are defined by descent. Their membranes do not lift them upward or let them circle above danger. They let a Kelden fall correctly: spreading broad limbs, riding the drop, crossing broken space, and landing where others would break.

Among Kelden, strength is measured by more than force. A strong person judges stone honestly, keeps calm when ash blinds the trail, and knows when to move before the ledge gives way.

## Basalt Bodies and Descent Membranes

Kelden are broad, heavy humanoids usually standing between 5½ and 7 feet tall. They have thick limbs, deep chests, powerful shoulders, strong hands, and wide gripping feet. Their movement is deliberate and grounded, with each step placed as if the surface beneath them deserves attention.

Their outer skin is tough, flexible, and mineralized, ranging from ash gray and dull iron to dark brown, black basalt, or obsidian-black with a faint glassy sheen. Many show rust-red veining, pale ash scars, sulfur-yellow mottling, ember-dark freckles, or lighter mineral striations across the brow, shoulders, arms, back, and membranes. Older Kelden often become rougher and more weathered, with thicker ridges and scarred surfaces from hard landings, ash abrasion, and falling stone.

A Kelden may resemble carved basalt at a glance, but they are living people. Their skin flexes, bruises, scars, sheds fine grit, and heals around injury. They breathe, bleed, eat, sleep, age, and raise children as other peoples do. Their faces are serious and strongly nonhuman, with heavy brows, deep-set eyes, broad breathing ridges, and mouths framed by tough dark tissue. Their voices often carry a low rasp or grinding resonance.

The most distinctive feature of a Kelden is the set of integrated descent membranes running along the arms, sides, hips, and legs. When folded, these membranes lie in heavy pleats along the body. When spread, they form a broad ribbed surface supported by muscle and mineralized struts. A Kelden must spread their limbs and commit their whole body to use them. Their hands remain functional for tools, weapons, shields, climbing, and spellcasting, but a true descent requires space, attention, and a fall.

Kelden hands and feet end in stone-grip claws. These are not talons made for tearing prey. They are practical gripping structures, useful for rough basalt, masonry, ledges, ladders, tools, and emergency holds when a path begins to crumble.

## Clothing and Equipment

Kelden clothing is built for abrasion, ash, weight, and membranes. Common garments include wrapped sashes, vented tunics, reinforced belts, work aprons, heavy sandals, ash masks, water skins, and tool harnesses. Loose cloth that can catch on stone or tangle in a membrane is treated as poor judgment, not style.

Armor and fitted clothing must account for a Kelden’s descent membranes, broad frame, dense body, claws, and unusual proportions. In communities familiar with Kelden bodies, this does not normally change the cost of ordinary gear. Rare, rushed, foreign, ceremonial, or specialized work may cost more at the DM’s discretion.

Light and medium armor can be adapted to leave a Kelden’s descent membranes functional. Heavy armor always blocks them. A Kelden in heavy armor can still climb, fight, cast spells, and travel normally, but cannot use Descent Membranes.

## Ashspine and Broken Ground

Ashspine is an island of black ridges, basalt shelves, ash valleys, sulfur fields, hot vents, cliff birds, ledge gardens, and settlements tucked against stone that can shelter or kill. Trails shift under ash, stone cracks after tremors, and safe routes are never trusted simply because they held yesterday.

Water is precious. Kelden settlements depend on covered cisterns, seep wells, guarded stores, and careful rationing. Food comes from hardy ledge crops, cliff-bird eggs, preserved meat, and trade for grain, oil, fruit, cloth, and goods Ashspine cannot reliably provide. The island exports basalt, obsidian, sulfur, pumice, volcanic glass, ores, stonework, and expertise in dangerous terrain.

The sea is less familiar than the cliffs. Kelden sailors exist, but deep water is serious business for a people who sink easily. Their vessels favor reinforced decks, strong handholds, flotation gear, and careful crews.

## Shelters, Judgment, and Rescue

Kelden communities are organized around shelters, cisterns, descent-lines, and the routes between them. A settlement may have elders, route keepers, water guardians, descent instructors, masons, vent watchers, or rescue hands, but authority often follows immediate hazard judgment. When stone fails, people listen to whoever best understands where it will go.

Daily life is full of small disciplines. Cistern covers are cleared. Descent lines are inspected. Food stores are sealed against ash. Children are taught where not to stand. A careless step can endanger the person below as much as the person who falls, so Kelden treat hazard awareness as a civic duty.

Rescue work carries deep honor. A Kelden who descends through falling ash, crosses broken stone, or braces a failing path long enough for others to pass earns more respect than one who merely survives alone. Kelden admire strength, but they admire controlled strength more. Panic, boasting, and reckless confidence are dangerous. Calm competence keeps people alive.

## Life Cycle and Family Structure

Kelden are live-born after long pregnancies. Infants are softer and lighter than adults, with flexible membranes, small gripping claws, and skin that darkens and mineralizes as they grow. Childhood is long, supervised, and full of lessons about footing, ash, ledges, water, and weight.

Families are often built around shelters, descent-lines, water stores, and work groups. The people who maintain your route, guard your cistern, and answer when you call from below may be kin in every meaningful sense.

Adulthood is usually marked by a public act of responsibility: completing a supervised descent, repairing a dangerous path, guarding water, joining a rescue, or taking duty for a shelter, route, or store. The meaning is consistent. A Kelden adult is someone whose weight can be trusted.

## Adventurers

Kelden leave Ashspine for practical reasons more often than romantic ones. Some travel to earn wealth for a damaged cistern, replace a collapsed shelter, repay a rescue debt, escort trade, learn foreign stonework, or secure tools and food for their people. Others serve as guides, masons, vent scouts, caravan guards, ruin delvers, cliff messengers, or envoys.

Some become adventurers because they failed a judgment and cannot yet return home. A route collapsed. A warning came too late. A rescue reached only some of those below. Others leave because Ashspine’s caution feels too narrow, and they want to learn what strength means where roads are flat, buildings are soft, and water is everywhere.

A Kelden adventurer might be an ashfall survivor, cliff rescuer, broken-ridge exile, sulfur-field laborer, ledge gardener, stone mason, water-store guardian, descent instructor, ruin scout, caravan shield, egg harvester, bridge tester, or traveler seeking a danger large enough to prove their judgment.

## How Other Peoples View Kelden

Other peoples often notice Kelden first as weight: the sound of their steps on a dock, the way floorboards complain beneath them, the care they take before entering a boat, or the ease with which they hold position against wind and shove. In ordinary rooms, this can make them seem awkward or severe. In dangerous terrain, the impression changes quickly.

Many islanders respect Kelden as rescuers, stoneworkers, vent scouts, route readers, and people who can keep judgment when the ground itself becomes unreliable. Builders respect their sense for load and fracture. Sailors may be wary of them around small craft, narrow gangplanks, and crowded decks.

Some outsiders mistake Kelden stillness for stubbornness or their deliberate speech for coldness. Kelden, in turn, often find lighter peoples careless with footing, shelter, warning signs, and weight. The best friendships form when both sides recognize the same impulse beneath different habits: the desire to bring companions through danger alive.

## Names

Kelden names are short, hard, and built to carry through ash masks, wind, and distance. Many use clipped vowels, breath stops, and clustered consonants such as kr, gr, dr, sk, th, vr, and zh. Most Kelden use a personal name followed by a ridge, shelter, descent-line, mineral mark, or earned work-name.

**Personal Names:** Brak, Drel, Harvek, Korga, Marn, Skavra, Thorek, Vadrik, Grelka, Drunn, Korveth, Haska, Zhur, Vraska, Tharnok, Velk, Grond, Reshka

**Ridge and Shelter Names:** Ashlip, Blackvein, Cinderfall, Deepvent, Glassridge, Redvent, Shattercliff, Skarnhold, Smoke Hollow, Sulfur Gate, Old Drop, Broken Shelf

**Work-Names and Earned Names:** Ash Reader, Seep Checker, Cliff Brace, Descent Hand, Drop Reader, Line Setter, Ridge Scout, Seep Warden, Shelter Mason, Stonehand, Vent Watch, Warning Voice

Examples include Harvek Blackvein, Vraska of Redvent, Drel Line Setter, Skavra Ash Reader, Korga of Smoke Hollow, and Thorek, Warning Voice of Shattercliff.

## Kelden Traits

Your Kelden character has traits shaped by a dense mineralized body, volcanic cliffs, ash-filled air, stone-grip claws, and broad descent membranes.

- **Ability Score Increase.** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Constitution score increases by 1.
- **Age.** Kelden mature slowly, reaching adulthood around age 25. They commonly live 120 to 180 years, though dangerous terrain and hard labor can shorten this.
- **Size.** Kelden are broad, dense humanoids usually standing between 5½ and 7 feet tall. Your size is Medium.
- **Speed.** Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
- **Creature Type.** You are a Humanoid.
- **Heavy Frame.** Your dense body is difficult to displace. While you are standing on stone, earth, or another solid surface that can support your weight, you have advantage on saving throws and ability checks made to resist being shoved, knocked prone, pushed, pulled, or otherwise moved against your will.
- **Descent Membranes.** When you fall at least 10 feet and are not incapacitated, restrained, wearing heavy armor, or in a space too narrow to spread your membranes, you can use your reaction to spread your descent membranes. When you do, you take no falling damage from that fall, and you can move horizontally up to 2 feet for every 1 foot you descend.

  This movement cannot make you gain altitude, hover, remain airborne, or travel farther than the fall allows. You must land in a space that can support you. If you cannot land in such a space, you fall normally from the point where your descent can no longer continue.

  Heavy armor prevents this trait from functioning.
- **Stone-Grip Claws.** You have advantage on Strength (Athletics) checks made to climb natural stone, volcanic rock, cliff faces, basalt ridges, masonry, and similar hard, rough surfaces.

  Your claws do not grant a natural weapon attack, and you do not have a climbing speed.
- **Volcanic Grounding.** You have advantage on saving throws against nonmagical smoke, ash inhalation, sulfurous fumes, airborne volcanic grit, and similar environmental breathing hazards.

  You also have advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks made to assess, navigate, or avoid natural hazards involving cliffs, loose stone, ash slopes, vents, unstable ledges, and similar volcanic terrain.
- **Poor Swimmer.** You have disadvantage on Strength (Athletics) checks made to swim, and you sink in water unless you are actively swimming or supported by flotation. Armor, heavy gear, exhaustion, rough water, or strong currents may make swimming impossible at the DM’s discretion.
- **Body-Plan Rulings.** Kelden have broadly humanoid frames, but their descent membranes, dense bodies, claws, and unusual proportions affect gear. Armor and clothing must be adapted around their membranes so they can move their arms, sides, hips, and legs properly. This does not normally change cost in communities familiar with Kelden bodies, but rare, rushed, foreign, or specialized work may cost more at the DM’s discretion.

  You use weapons, shields, tools, spellcasting focuses, and magic items normally when they are adapted to your body. Light and medium armor can be adapted to leave your descent membranes functional. Heavy armor always prevents you from using Descent Membranes. Most standard mounts cannot comfortably carry a Kelden without special training or equipment.
- **Languages.** You can speak, read, and write Common and Kelden. Kelden is a compressed language of clipped vowels, heavy consonants, breath stops, and low resonant rhythms, shaped to carry through ash masks, wind, and distance.
