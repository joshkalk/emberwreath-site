# Zivari

**Homeland:** Saffra

The leaves were beautiful, the jars were sealed, and the balcony was full of buyers. Rasil of Pepperglass spread his throat fan until it shone like red oil in the sun.

“You have seen the harvest,” he called. “Now ask what your own ships are worthy to carry.”

Below him, Miren Clear-Eye lifted one drying tray, turned three leaves between her fingers, and set it down.

Rasil’s fan folded halfway.

“The third rack waits,” he said. “These are not ready.”

## Quick Children of Warm Stone

The Zivari are small, smooth-scaled reptilian people shaped by the warm terraces of Saffra. Their homeland is a wealthy southern island of spices, teas, medicinal flowers, citrus, rare herbs, aromatic oils, fragrant woods, grafted orchards, drying courts, market balconies, and cultivated prosperity.

Zivari are terrace runners, crop assessors, spice handlers, roof-crossers, ledger keepers, public speakers, herbalists, duelists, couriers, cooks, scouts, priests, traders, and adventurers. They are known for speed, balance, careful hands, sharp eyes, and a culture where display must be backed by substance.

To a Zivari, a bright throat fan, a crowded drying court, a famous Terrace mark, a sealed jar, or a well-kept nursery all make the same claim: we can sustain this.

## Smooth Scales, Bright Fans, and Narrow Tails

Zivari are compact reptilian humanoids, usually between 3½ and 5 feet tall. Their bodies are springy and quick rather than strong. They have smooth scales, narrow hands, gripping toe pads, alert eyes, and long tapering tails used for balance, braking, posture, quick turns, and social expression.

Most Zivari stand and walk upright in ordinary life. When speed matters, many drop low or move briefly on all fours, using their hands, feet, toe pads, and tails to skim across warm stone, tiled roofs, orchard walls, irrigation ledges, and crowded drying courts. Their arms are not wings and their tails are not grasping limbs, but their whole body is built for quick motion across worked terrain.

Zivari show clear sexual dimorphism. Male Zivari are usually more brightly colored, often marked with saffron yellow, rust red, orange, cream, blue-gray, charcoal, black edging, or vivid speckling. Most male Zivari have expandable throat fans that fold beneath the jaw and throat. These fans are biological display structures used in courtship, rivalry, festival presentation, market speech, public emphasis, and visible claim-making.

Female Zivari usually lack throat fans and are often more muted in coloration. Their scales tend toward warm brown, olive, pale cream, clay, sand, gray-green, charcoal, muted rust, or subtle bands and speckles. Their authority is carried through stillness, eye focus, tail control, precise posture, and judgment.

On Saffra, a fan can draw the crowd, but a still gaze can stop the sale.

## Clothing and Equipment

Zivari dress for heat, speed, work, and presentation. Common clothing includes open-throat vests, short work breeches, tail-split trousers, light wraps, market aprons, open-toed grip sandals, shoulder cloths, narrow belts, and fitted pouches that stay secure during sudden movement.

Male clothing usually leaves the throat fan free to fold, breathe, and spread. Festival garments, speaker’s vests, and courtship clothing often frame the fan without covering it. Female clothing often favors clean lines, close fits, careful tailoring, and controlled ornament, though bright color and bold marks are hardly male possessions. Any Zivari may use cloth, trade beads, Terrace clasps, painted marks, or careful grooming to make a public claim.

Zivari wealth is usually shown through useful excellence: a superior ledger case, engraved trade weights, polished crop knives, sealed sample tubes, aromatic wood beads, fine stitching, or a perfectly fitted work vest. A well-dressed Zivari should still be able to climb a stair wall, inspect a drying tray, sprint across tile, and bargain under the sun.

Zivari can use weapons, shields, tools, spellcasting focuses, and magic items normally. Armor must be cut to account for the tail, and male Zivari armor or collars must leave the throat fan able to fold safely.

## Saffra and the Terraces

Saffra is a warm southern island of terraced hillsides, orchard paths, spice houses, drying courts, mist-catching ridges, market balconies, old stone stairs, roof paths, fragrant workshops, and carefully guarded water. Its wealth comes from refinement rather than bulk. Breadbasket feeds crowds. Saffra sells things small enough to lock in a chest and valuable enough to change a family’s future.

A Zivari Terrace is more than a farm. It may include planted slopes, crop walls, family rooms, nursery rooms, apprentice beds, worker kitchens, drying courts, water channels, storage vaults, roof paths, ledger rooms, market stalls, and trade seals. It is home, business, workshop, reputation, and productive capacity in one place.

The great Terraces of Saffra are famous for high-value crops whose quality can be ruined by one careless afternoon. Zivari children often learn to judge scent, color, weight, dryness, bruising, and fraud before they are trusted with a blade or a ledger.

Modern Saffra is not Zivari-only. Other peoples work its terraces, own shops, captain ships, manage water, broker contracts, serve in guard companies, tend crops, and build lives there. The Zivari remain deeply tied to Saffra’s terrace culture, but Saffra’s prosperity depends on many hands.

## Productive Proof

The Zivari value prosperity, but they do not respect idleness. A wealthy child is expected to learn ledgers, crop timing, water schedules, grafting, quality grading, market speech, storage discipline, apprenticeship obligations, spouse-circle duties, and the cost of false display.

A Terrace earns status by proving capacity. It must support workers, honor contracts, maintain irrigation shares, train apprentices, raise children, protect crops, repay debts, and make its public marks match reality. A Terrace that spreads its seal across goods it cannot guarantee invites shame.

This value shapes Zivari manners. Boasting is acceptable if the proof is ready. Confidence is admired if the work can bear it. Public correction is painful, but useful.

Plural spouse-circles are common among established Terraces, though practices vary by region, wealth, and family history. Among Zivari, plurality is not supposed to signal indulgence. It signals capacity: more adults, more children, more alliances, more labor, and more obligations.

## Clutches and Family

Zivari are egg-laying humanoids. A female Zivari usually lays one clutch during her life, most often two to four eggs. Producing a clutch is physically demanding and requires long recovery. Some females never lay eggs. A few lay more than once under unusually favorable conditions, but this is uncommon.

Zivari eggs take close to two years to hatch. They are kept in warm nursery rooms, sun-warmed walls, guarded incubation chambers, or other carefully tended spaces within a Terrace or household.

Zivari hatch with their basic body plan already formed: arms, legs, tail, scales, eyes, gripping pads, and the movement pattern that will shape adult life. Male throat fans develop later. Female Zivari do not develop throat fans.

Children from the same clutch are called clutch-siblings. They are usually raised together, trained together, compared together, and remembered together. Clutch-siblings may become close allies, rivals, business partners, advocates, or competitors for the same Terrace role.

Zivari families can be large, practical, affectionate, demanding, and intensely watchful. A child’s first lessons often involve carrying samples, sorting leaves, balancing on warm walls, reading posture, and listening during bargains.

## Adventurers

Zivari adventurers leave Saffra for many reasons. Some proudly carry a Terrace mark into the wider world. Others leave because they are tired of being measured by one. A Zivari might adventure to found a Terrace, restore a failed claim, escape an unwanted spouse-circle, settle a clutch-sibling rivalry, expose fraud, cure a blighted crop, repay a debt, or prove a skill no one at home respected.

Zivari can belong to any class. Their speed, balance, public force, careful hands, and attention to proof can suit many callings, from scouts and duelists to priests, scholars, mages, traders, and wandering guards. A Zivari adventurer does not need to be a merchant. They need only understand that claims have weight.

## How Other Peoples View Zivari

Other peoples often see Zivari as quick, polished, prosperous, sharp-eyed, and demanding. Saffran goods carry prestige across much of the archipelago, and Zivari traders, assessors, herbalists, cooks, and brokers are often welcomed when quality matters.

That welcome can come with irritation. Some find Zivari status-conscious, theatrical, suspiciously exact, or too willing to turn a casual promise into a measurable obligation.

Axali often understand Zivari presentation better than most, though they may find it too practical and too tied to production. To an Axali, elegance can be an art of reputation. To a Zivari, presentation without capacity is a cracked jar with fresh paint.

The Zivari answer judgment plainly: test the seal, smell the leaves, count the jars, inspect the ledgers, walk the terrace, and see what lasts.

## Names

Zivari names often favor sibilants, liquid sounds, and smooth vowels. S, Z, R, L, V, M, T, and soft K sounds are common, especially with A, E, and O sounds. Names tend to feel quick, sun-warmed, and polished rather than harsh, chittering, or draconic.

Most Zivari use a personal name followed by a Terrace name, such as Rasil of Pepperglass or Miren of Clearcourt. In formal settings, a Zivari may add a work title, spouse-circle role, earned name, or public mark.

**Male Names:** Rasil, Zethro, Serren, Varo, Lethan, Morro, Kazel, Zarro, Tessir, Zairo

**Female Names:** Miren, Zalia, Ressai, Liora, Tazri, Kessa, Velin, Sorai, Marra, Zelith

**Terrace Names:** Pepperglass, Clearcourt, Goldleaf, Sunwall, Redtile, Oiltree, Brightcourt, High Peel, Bitterflower, Greenroof, Glass Orchard, Warm Steps

Examples include Rasil Brightfan of Pepperglass, Miren Clear-Eye of Clearcourt, Zalia Trueweight of Goldleaf, or Liora Still-Tail, assessor of Glass Orchard.

## Zivari Traits

Your Zivari character has traits shaped by smooth scales, warm-stone movement, terrace culture, quick hands, careful balance, and the pressure of public proof.

- **Ability Score Increase.** Male and female Zivari usually develop along different physical and social lines.

  **Male Zivari:** Your Dexterity score increases by 1, and your Charisma score increases by 2.

  **Female Zivari:** Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

  Most Zivari follow these patterns, but individual variation exists. With the DM’s permission, a Zivari whose body, upbringing, or role differs from the usual pattern may use either Zivari ability score option.
- **Age.** Zivari eggs take close to two years to hatch. Zivari reach adulthood around age 16 and commonly live 60 to 80 years.
- **Size.** Zivari are small, compact reptilian humanoids usually standing between 3½ and 5 feet tall. Your size is Small.
- **Speed.** Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
- **Creature Type.** You are a Humanoid.
- **Warm-Stone Agility.** When you take the Dash action, you can ignore nonmagical difficult terrain caused by stairs, terraces, narrow ledges, loose gravel, market clutter, irrigation channels, tiled roofs, orchard rows, uneven stonework, or similar cultivated terrain until the end of the turn.

  You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Cool-Blooded.** You have disadvantage on saving throws against exhaustion caused by extreme cold.
- **Fine Hands.** You gain proficiency in one of the following of your choice: Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, Performance, cook’s utensils, herbalism kit, jeweler’s tools, mason’s tools, or calligrapher’s supplies.
- **Tail Balance.** When you fall, you can reduce any falling damage by an amount equal to five times your proficiency bonus. You also have advantage on saving throws made to avoid falling prone because of slipping, narrow footing, loose gravel, unstable ledges, terrace paths, or similar ground.
- **Body-Plan Rulings.** Zivari can wear normal armor if it accommodates their tail and, for males, leaves the throat fan free enough to fold and display. They use weapons, shields, tools, spellcasting focuses, and magic items normally. Their tails are not prehensile, and throat fans are display structures with no independent mechanical function.
- **Languages.** You can speak, read, and write Common and Zivari. Zivari is a quick language of sibilants, smooth vowels, clipped breath sounds, and precise social emphasis. Tail posture and throat-fan display can color face-to-face speech, but the spoken language does not require them.
