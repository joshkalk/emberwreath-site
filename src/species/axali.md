# Axali

**Homeland:** Hollowdeep

When Lady Berathina appeared at the Mirrored Baths wearing a single ribbon of Cobalt Veil, three houses took it for a declaration and a fourth called it an insult. She declined to explain herself. The next morning, when a collapsed passage trapped six dyers below the steam galleries, she tied the same ribbon around her sword arm and entered the flooded dark.

## Grace Beneath the Surface

The Axali are graceful freshwater amphibious people shaped by clear springs, grotto halls, baths, pigments, textiles, and the discipline of public presentation. Their ancestral home of Hollowdeep looks dry and severe from the sea: pale karst ridges, black volcanic stone, sparse scrub, dusty roads, and sinkholes opening into shadow. Beneath that surface lies a different world of cenotes, mineral pools, hidden rivers, lanternlit bath courts, steam gardens, textile houses, and humid estates built around clean water.

Axali life is famous across the archipelago for vivid fabrics, formal hospitality, subtle courtship customs, and a social world where color, posture, stillness, and grooming can matter as much as spoken words. This refinement is not empty vanity. In Hollowdeep, being seen has consequences.

Axali are not only nobles and courtiers. Their people include dyers, bath workers, attendants, scribes, guards, merchants, performers, scholars, duelists, healers, servants, and adventurers. What unites them is not rank, but training in how a person carries themself when others are watching.

## Clear Skin, Living Color

Axali are slender upright amphibians, generally comparable in height to humans but narrower through the shoulders, ribs, and hips. Their smooth, moisture-sensitive skin appears in pale shades of pearl, cream, shell pink, blush ivory, pale blue-gray, champagne, or soft lavender. They have long limbs, lightly webbed hands and feet, and narrow flattened tails that aid balance and movement through clean water.

Their dark reflective eyes are larger than human eyes and well suited to the dim pools and limestone galleries of Hollowdeep. From each side of the head extend three delicate branching frills, softly translucent and naturally touched with rose, peach, pale gold, silver-blue, violet-gray, or faint green. Axali frills are living structures, sensitive to dryness, damage, mood, and embarrassment. Most Axali learn early to protect them, frame them, and control the signals they send.

Axali bodies carry color subtly, so added color matters. Pigment at the brow, throat, wrist, shoulder, frill base, or tail edge may express mourning, hospitality, artistic allegiance, household ties, courtship interest, professional standing, or private defiance. Stillness is also a language. An Axali who lowers their frills, smooths their sleeve, and says nothing may be showing more than anger.

## Clothing and Equipment

Axali have broadly humanoid frames. They can use ordinary armor, clothing, weapons, shields, tools, spellcasting focuses, and magic items normally. Most armor and clothing made for them is adjusted around the head frills, tail, moisture wraps, and display surfaces used for pigment or ornament.

Axali formal dress favors layered wraps, fine draping, short mantles, fitted vests, damp inner cloth, elegant sandals, jewelry, scent, and visible pigment work. Practical travelers use simpler garments: hooded moisture cloaks, sealable waterskins, soft wrappings for the frills, protective gloves, and pigment kits packed in lacquered cases. An Axali adventurer may look courtly even on the road, but the polish is often practical. Clean cloth protects the skin, and a composed appearance can open doors before steel is drawn.

## Hollowdeep and Its Waters

Hollowdeep is an old volcanic island mantled in pale limestone and carved from within by fresh water. Underground rivers feed cenotes, spring chambers, grotto estates, gardens, and public baths. Surface settlements handle docks, roads, trade yards, and dryland labor, but Hollowdeep’s celebrated districts descend below the island into cool halls of water and stone.

The great baths began as places of Axali health and community. Over time, they became the island’s defining institution: immersion pools, mineral baths, steam rooms, resting galleries, music chambers, reception salons, fencing courts, medical pools, and carefully tended gardens. Visitors from across the archipelago come to negotiate, recover, court, perform, commission garments, or be seen in the right place at the right hour.

Hollowdeep’s wealth rests on textiles, pigments, dyes, cosmetics, rinses, inks, and formal clothing. Its artisans import fibers, minerals, oils, shells, plant matter, and rare powders, then transform them into the archipelago’s most desired fabrics and colors. Axali fashion travels widely, adapted for many bodies and customs, but its source remains unmistakable: clean water, careful hands, and a society trained to read surfaces.

Clean water is sacred in the practical sense. Springs are guarded. Pools are maintained. Rinsing customs are enforced. Brine, sewage, ash-heavy runoff, and polluted cisterns are dangerous to Axali bodies and offensive to Axali sensibilities. To waste clear water is shameful. To poison it is close to unforgivable.

## Society, Communities, and Values

Hollowdeep has no single king. Its public life is shaped by houses, guilds, bath courts, patronage networks, artisan lineages, wealthy families, and old reputations. Some Great Houses are ancient Axali lineages with estates built around private springs. Others rose from textile workshops, bath service, trade, scholarship, or performance. Established families of other peoples can also hold power on Hollowdeep, though Axali custom remains the island’s social grammar.

Axali value composure, hospitality, skill, careful speech, clean water, artistic discipline, and the ability to understand what others are trying not to say. A good host protects a guest’s dignity. A good guest does not make the host’s generosity look foolish. A good artist knows when a color should speak loudly and when it should barely be seen.

That pressure can be beautiful, but it can also be cruel. Hollowdeep remembers scandals. Families arrange introductions with strategy. A public mistake may be forgiven in words and repeated in whispers. Many Axali learn to hide hurt behind perfect posture. Others reject the game, or try to master it well enough to survive without being owned by it.

## Life Cycle and Family Structure

Axali children develop from internally brooded eggs and are born in prepared freshwater nursery pools, usually in small numbers. Their earliest months are spent in protected water under the care of parents, kin, attendants, and trained pool keepers. As they grow, they spend more time on land, learning speech, balance, etiquette, water care, and the first meanings of color.

Axali reach adulthood around age 18 and commonly live 80 to 120 years. Families may be intimate households, large estates, artisan lines, bath-service clans, or merchant families with apprentices and wards. Marriage is socially important because it joins households and clarifies inheritance, obligations, and continuity. Interspecies affection and lifelong companionship exist in cosmopolitan Hollowdeep, but marriage between different species is not socially recognized, and no hybrid children are possible.

Adoption and wardship are respected ways to continue a household, protect a child, repay a debt, or recognize talent. A dyer’s ward may inherit secret formulas. A bath house may raise a child into service and later patronage. A disgraced noble child may find a truer family among performers, guards, scribes, or travelers.

## Adventurers

Axali leave Hollowdeep for ambition, duty, scandal, scholarship, craft, love, exile, or relief from being constantly interpreted. Some seek rare dyes, fibers, minerals, fragrances, shells, or freshwater plants that could make their work famous. Others serve a house as agents, couriers, duelists, guards, investigators, or negotiators. Still others travel to protect springs, settle family debts, recover stolen pigments, or prove that their worth extends beyond polished rooms.

An Axali adventurer might be a formal duelist, bath-trained healer, traveling dyer, pigment maker, house envoy, disgraced heir, social investigator, scholar of underground rivers, merchant’s guard, performer, spy, or runaway court favorite. Some remain deeply proud of Hollowdeep. Others carry their homeland like a mirror they are tired of facing.

On the road, Axali often struggle less with danger than with disorder: dirty water, brine-stiff clothing, ash storms, open mockery, and companions who say exactly what they mean in public. Still, many become excellent adventurers. They read rooms quickly, prepare carefully, and know that appearances can be armor.

## How Other Peoples View Axali

Many outsiders admire Axali elegance, craft, and hospitality. Hollowdeep cloth, dyes, and bath customs are markers of wealth across the archipelago. Diplomats prize Axali attendants and interpreters because they notice small shifts in mood before negotiations break.

Others distrust them. Axali manners can seem indirect, judgmental, expensive, or manipulative to people who value blunt speech. Water-poor communities may resent Hollowdeep’s baths, even when Axali insist that the pools are maintained, reused, and central to health. Seafaring peoples sometimes mistake Axali for a broader waterfolk, then learn that brine is not their comfort and the open sea is not their home.

Aumari often see Axali as delicate but dangerously perceptive. Scolen may respect their water care while finding their social customs inefficient. Humans tend to admire Hollowdeep fashion and underestimate how much discipline sits beneath it. Ferren often prefer an Axali who can stop performing and speak plainly.

## Names

Axali formal names are long, crisp, and carefully articulated. Full names are used for introductions, invitations, contracts, ceremonies, and public apologies. Short forms imply familiarity, affection, patronage, or presumption. Using one too soon can be insulting.

Most Axali also carry a household, estate, bath, or artisan name. A person may be introduced through a Great House, a respected workshop, a bath court, or a family line known for pigment, textiles, scholarship, or service.

**Feminine Names:** Abrelithia, Berenatha, Caladria, Dorethessa, Maribetta, Talvoria

**Masculine Names:** Adrobetan, Baritheon, Coradivar, Delvoret, Tabrion, Valtamer

**Familiar Forms:** Abri, Bera, Cala, Dori, Litha, Tavi, Baro, Corin, Del, Valt

**Household Names:** Belcadran, Corthabel, Maridane, Talcorith, Veradine, Watrellon

**Estates and Baths:** Stillglass Grotto, Pearlwater Court, the Mirrored Baths, Rosewater Terrace, the Steam Garden, Blue Lantern Pools

## Axali Traits

Your Axali character has traits shaped by a freshwater amphibian body, dim limestone grottoes, and a culture of cultivated presentation.

- **Ability Score Increase.** Your Charisma score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.
- **Age.** Axali spend their earliest lives in protected freshwater nursery pools. They reach adulthood around age 18 and commonly live 80 to 120 years.
- **Size.** Axali are slender humanoids generally comparable in height to humans. Your size is Medium.
- **Speed.** Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
- **Creature Type.** You are a Humanoid.
- **Freshwater Amphibian.** You can breathe air and fresh water. You have a swimming speed of 30 feet while swimming in fresh water. This trait grants no ability to breathe seawater, brine, sewage, polluted water, or similarly contaminated water, and your swimming speed from this trait does not function in such water.
- **Grotto Eyes.** In dim light within 60 feet of you, you can see as if it were bright light. This trait does not allow you to see in darkness.
- **Moisture-Bound Skin.** You must refresh your skin with clean freshwater once every 24 hours by thoroughly rinsing or bathing, spending at least 1 hour in a clean freshwater-humid environment, or wearing clean freshwater-soaked wraps for at least 1 hour.

  If you fail to do so, you have disadvantage on saving throws against exhaustion caused by dehydration, extreme heat, or environmental dryness until you refresh your skin. If you spend 1 hour or more submerged in seawater, brine, sewage, polluted water, or similarly contaminated water, you must refresh your skin with clean freshwater before completing your next long rest or suffer the same disadvantage until you do.
- **Cultivated Presentation.** You gain proficiency in one of the following skills of your choice: History, Insight, Performance, or Persuasion.
- **Textile and Pigment Tradition.** You gain proficiency with either painter’s supplies or weaver’s tools. At the DM’s discretion, you can identify obvious signs of poor workmanship, imitation, damage, or exceptional quality in textiles, dyes, pigments, formal garments, or decorative body markings.
- **Color and Consequence.** When you spend at least 10 minutes preparing your appearance with pigment, grooming, clothing, jewelry, scent, or other presentation tools, choose one Charisma skill in which you are proficient. For the next hour, when you make an ability check using that skill in a formal, public, diplomatic, artistic, mercantile, or courtly setting, you can add a d4 to the roll.

  You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Languages.** You can speak, read, and write Common and Axali. Axali is precise and formal, with many terms for color, courtesy, public silence, and degrees of familiarity.
