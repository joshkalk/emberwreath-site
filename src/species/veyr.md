# Veyr

*The herd would not cross the white stones. The merchant cursed the delay, pointed to the green hollow beyond, and offered two silver more for haste. The Veyr guide turned their blank clay mask toward him, inner lights dim behind the eye openings, and rang one small bell at their wrist. Every goat knelt in the dust.*

*“There is grass beyond the stones,” the Veyr said. “There are also roads that should not be remembered aloud.”*

## Kindled Road-Witnesses

Kindled children of cord, clay, dust, and old pasture roads, the Veyr are a crafted people of Sunspire. They are living range-votives, made through careful communal rites from ceramic, cord, leather, range-cloth, bells, bronze fittings, bone tokens, old repairs, and inner light.

Veyr are people, uncanny in form and practical in life. They eat, drink, rest, learn, grieve, mend, remember, and choose. Though crafted rather than born, they are not property, tools, or servants.

Most Veyr understand themselves through roads. They guide herds between surviving pastures, tend markers beside dry gullies, watch old cistern approaches, judge when grazing land must recover, and stand before places that should not be reopened. A Veyr might explain their duty simply: “I was made to guide the living around places that should not be reopened.”

## Mask-Faces, Cords, and Inner Light

Veyr stand about 6 feet tall, though their height can be difficult to judge because they hover roughly four inches above solid ground. Their bodies are assembled rather than grown: ceramic plates, wrapped joints, corded seams, leather bindings, layered cloth, heavy foot-anchors, and old fittings held together by kindled craft and inner light.

A Veyr’s movement is quiet but not weightless. Their articulated limbs shift with the soft scrape of ceramic, cord, cloth, and leather. Bells, tokens, and small repair charms move with them as they pass. Their foot-anchors hang just above the ground, close enough to brush dust and grit, reminding observers that Veyr movement is still bound to the road.

Every Veyr is known by a ceramic mask-face. It is not decoration, and it does not hide another face beneath it. Some mask-faces are smooth clay ovals marked with route-band colors. Others carry bronze rims, repaired cracks, painted warning lines, ash stains, horn fittings, or patches of clay taken from a particular well or road shrine. Some bear deliberately blank places where a symbol has been erased and never replaced.

Behind the mask-face’s dark eye openings glow dim inner lights rather than ordinary eyes. Veyr do not blink. They have no visible mouths. Their voices come from resonant inner cords, shaped by the mask-face and torso chambers into a dry, solemn sound like air passing through ceramic.

Veyr eat and drink through a concealed intake slot in the torso, usually hidden beneath a mantle, wrap, or layered range-cloth. The slot is treated with privacy and practical care.

## Clothing and Equipment

Veyr clothing is built around access, repair, and identity. They favor mantles, sun-cloaks, corded harnesses, dust veils, bell-straps, tool loops, intake coverings, and reinforced pouches for repair materials. A well-kept Veyr is often recognized by tightened cords, brushed seams, oiled leather, clean intake cloth, and foot-anchors free of grit.

Their gear must account for a ceramic mask-face, broad mantle, articulated limbs, corded seams, foot-anchors, and intake slot, but Veyr use weapons, shields, tools, spellcasting focuses, armor, and magic items as other adventurers do.

## The Dry Roads of Sunspire

Sunspire is a dry island of ridges, mesas, ash plains, broken terraces, thorn scrub, cactus growth, hard seasonal grasses, abandoned orchards, old cisterns, and surviving strips of cultivation around wells and reservoirs. Its coasts support fishing, craftwork, and some trade, but the deep interior belongs to heat, dust, animals, and memory.

The Veyr are tied to that interior. They know herd paths, reservoir approaches, grazing circuits, dry gullies, cracked roads, boundary stones, and ruined stonework half-buried by ash. They remember where goats can pass, where carts should not be taken, where a marker has shifted, where a well has gone bitter, and where a green hollow is not worth the cost of reaching it.

Sunspire was once greater than it is now. Its ruined terraces, buried canals, weathered stonework, and silent inland boundaries speak of an older power whose full story is no longer told. The Veyr do not claim to rule that history. They tend its dangerous edges.

## Society, Communities, and Values

The Veyr have little formal government. They organize around practical bonds: route-bands, herd-lines, mask-kin, range-witnesses, and boundary-keepers.

A route-band may travel together for a season, tending markers, guiding animals, and checking whether roads remain safe. A herd-line preserves knowledge of animal movement, breeding, rest, and pasture recovery across years. Mask-kin are those who helped kindle, train, repair, or witness one another. These bonds can matter as deeply as family.

Veyr authority is rarely formal. It comes from witness. A Veyr who saw a marker moved, a spring fail, or a pasture stripped too early may be asked to speak before caravan leaders, herders, well-keepers, or local elders. Their word is not law everywhere on Sunspire. It carries weight because people remember what happens when it is ignored.

Their most serious duty is boundary-keeping. Across Sunspire’s interior stand stones, cords, faded warning marks, broken walls, and silent stretches of road that Veyr will not cross with animals or travelers. They may say a route is closed, a hollow is unfit for living passage, or a marker must be honored.

Their silence is not simple secrecy. Some knowledge tempts the living toward grief, greed, or old harm. Some silences preserve mercy toward the dead. A Veyr may refuse to speak because they know enough.

## Life Cycle and Family Structure

Veyr are not born. They are kindled through a communal process involving craft, memory, materials, food, water, and consent from those who will help sustain the new life.

A community does not kindle a Veyr lightly. To call another person into the world is to accept years of training, care, repair, and obligation. The work binds the new Veyr to their mask-kin, but it does not make them property. A kindled Veyr is a person from the moment they wake.

A newly kindled Veyr is not an infant, but neither are they an adult. They can move soon after kindling, and many learn speech quickly, but their first years are spent as novices. They learn how to settle their articulated bodies, tend animals, maintain their mask-faces, read road markers, avoid unsafe ruins, judge grazing conditions, and speak properly as witnesses.

Social adulthood usually comes after 8 to 12 years of training.

Veyr eat, drink, and rest like other peoples. Rest is often a still meditative settling, with the mask-face lowered, bells quieted, and cords loosened or retied. They also practice constant material upkeep: tightening knots, oiling leather, brushing dust from seams, checking foot-anchors, replacing worn cord, repairing chipped ceramic, and settling inner tokens.

A well-maintained Veyr commonly lives 100 to 150 years. Rare elders may live longer.

## Adventurers

Veyr adventurers leave Sunspire for duty, necessity, curiosity, exile, friendship, or unfinished witness. Some guide caravans through failing routes. Some pursue stolen route tokens, lost bells, missing herd animals, or travelers who vanished near old boundaries. Some seek tools, materials, magic, or knowledge needed to repair a mask-kin elder or preserve a fading inner light.

Others travel because Sunspire has become too narrow. A Veyr may question a silence without breaking it, seek friendship among peoples who do not know their duties, or discover that a road can become sacred only after one chooses to walk it.

A Veyr adventurer might be a caravan guide, range-witness, boundary-keeper, herd guardian, mask-mender, dryland scout, wandering mediator, animal handler, relic seeker, road cleric, ranger, druid, monk, or quiet pilgrim following a marker no one else can read.

## How Other Peoples View Veyr

Most people of Sunspire treat Veyr with wary respect. Herders value their judgment. Well-keepers listen when they speak of water. Caravans complain about their delays, then hire them again. Inland families may know a local Veyr by mask-face, bell, and route long before they know their full name.

Coastal Sunspire folk sometimes see Veyr as necessary but unsettling. A Veyr appearing at a port can mean trade, testimony, bad news from the interior, or a boundary problem no one wants to discuss.

Other islanders often mistake Veyr for relics, omens, or animated remains of a vanished age. Most Veyr correct this once. Those who travel with them usually learn a different truth: Veyr are practical companions, careful witnesses, dry humorists, patient animal handlers, and stubborn protectors of the living.

## Names

Veyr names are sexless and usually short, shaped by resonant cords and ceramic mask-faces. They favor short, dry-sounding names with soft breath sounds, low vowels, and sounds that carry clearly across open ground. A Veyr may have a kindled name, a route-band association, and one or more witness titles earned through work.

**Kindled Names:** Arvek, Besh, Darel, Eshun, Havar, Iral, Leth, Maivar, Nesh, Orrek, Ravel, Soth, Taren, Thoval, Uresh, Yarun

**Route-Band Names:** Ashmarker, Bellroad, Deep Cistern, Dry Orchard, Embergrass, Red Mesa, Saltstep, Sunhollow, Thornwait, White Boundary

**Witness Titles:** Range-Witness, Bell-Keeper, Marker-Mender, Cistern Guard, Herd-Caller, Boundary-Keeper, Road-Rememberer, Mask-Mender, Pasture Judge, Well-Watcher

Examples include Eshun of Ashmarker Route, Thoval Bell-Keeper, Maivar of White Boundary, Ravel the Mask-Mender, or simply Besh.

## Veyr Traits

Your Veyr character has traits shaped by a kindled body, dry pastoral roads, warning boundaries, and the old routes of Sunspire.

- **Ability Score Increase.** Your Wisdom score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.
- **Age.** Veyr are physically functional soon after kindling, but they spend years as novices learning movement, speech, upkeep, animal care, route memory, and boundary custom. They usually reach social adulthood 8 to 12 years after kindling. Well-maintained Veyr commonly live 100 to 150 years, with rare elders living longer.
- **Size.** Veyr are usually around 6 feet tall, though their hovering stance can make them seem slightly taller or shorter. Your size is Medium.
- **Speed.** Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
- **Creature Type.** You are a humanoid.
- **Surface-Bound Hover.** You hover roughly 4 inches above solid ground or a load-bearing constructed surface. This movement counts as walking, not flying.

  You cannot stand or travel on water or other liquids, cross open air, cross pits, cross chasms, move over unsupported gaps, climb vertical surfaces, or bypass terrain in any way a walking creature could not. You fall normally if there is no solid support beneath you.

  Nonmagical difficult terrain caused by loose dust, shallow ash, dry sand, gravel, scree, broken road, rubble, herd-trampled ground, or ruined stonework does not cost you extra movement. You also have advantage on ability checks and saving throws you make to avoid slipping, falling prone, or losing your footing on such ground.
- **Range-Witness.** You gain proficiency in your choice of Animal Handling or Survival.

  In addition, you have advantage on Wisdom (Animal Handling) checks you make to calm, guide, tend, or assess domesticated herd animals or pack animals.
- **Road Memory.** You can accurately recall overland routes, grazing circuits, road marker sequences, and reservoir approaches you have traveled within the past year.

  You have advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks you make to retrace such a route, recognize moved markers along it, or notice that a familiar path has become unsafe.
- **Marker Sense.** You are practiced at reading small signs of passage, repair, warning, and disturbance on dry roads and ruin-strewn ground.

  When you make an Intelligence (Investigation) or Wisdom (Survival) check to examine road markers, boundary stones, broken roads, old cistern approaches, dry gullies, ash, scree, rubble, ruins, recently used routes, moved markers, unsafe ground, or signs of herd or caravan passage, you can add a d4 to the roll. You can use this trait after seeing the roll but before knowing whether it succeeds or fails.
- **Tangle-Prone Frame.** Your corded seams, wrapped joints, and foot-anchors can catch on restraints. You have disadvantage on ability checks made to escape nets, ropes, snares, brambles, hooked chains, or similar entangling restraints.
- **Unusual Frame.** You have no visible mouth. You speak through resonant inner cords and eat and drink through a concealed intake slot in your torso.

  Armor, clothing, and fitted gear must be adapted for your ceramic mask-face, mantle, articulated limbs, corded seams, foot-anchors, and intake slot. This does not normally change the cost of such gear in communities familiar with Veyr bodies, but rare, rushed, foreign, or highly specialized work may cost more at the DM’s discretion.

  You use weapons, shields, tools, spellcasting focuses, and magic items normally when they are adapted to your body.
- **Languages.** You can speak, read, and write Common and Veyr. Veyr is a resonant language shaped by inner cords, ceramic mask-faces, low vowels, soft fricatives, bell cues, and stillness.
