# Island Quick Reference

Use this page to skim possible character origins without reading full island gazetteers. These cards give player-facing basics: what a place is known for, who commonly lives there, and why someone might leave.

Not every island or region is equally suitable as a homeland. Some are ordinary homes, some are frontier or seasonal origins, and some are danger regions better used as warnings, scars, losses, taboos, or backstory pressure.

## Homeland islands

<div class="card-grid">
  <section class="card">
    <h2>Breadbasket</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Human homeland and agricultural core.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Roads, granaries, mills, ports, crowns, and food politics.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Humans are dominant; all species are common because food and institutions pull people in.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Farmers, mill families, road workers, soldiers, clerks, nobles, convoy guards, or people tied to harvest duty and food distribution.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Obligation, ambition, service, politics, debt, convoy work, or family pressure.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Saffra</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Zivari homeland of careful cultivation and public reputation.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Warm terraces, orchards, medicines, dyes, spices, and luxury goods.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Zivari are dominant, with merchants and visitors in trade centers.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Cultivators, medicine keepers, terrace workers, traders, public performers, warehouse families, or people tied to reputation.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Trade, apprenticeship, proof, debt, crop crisis, family expectation, or rare-material work.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Sunspire</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Veyr homeland shaped by dry routes and old warnings.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Boundary markers, reservoirs, herding lands, new coastal trade life, and forbidden ruins.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Veyr are dominant; outsiders are uncommon.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Route witnesses, herd guides, cistern tenders, boundary keepers, coastal traders, or people pulled between warnings and trade.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Guide work, witness duty, trade, pilgrimage, drought messages, or because a boundary, route, or warning matters elsewhere.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Greenwood</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Ferren homeland of cold forests and northern frontier life.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Timber roads, harbors, ropeways, holds, old groves, and ship timber.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Ferren are dominant, with migrants and traders near ports.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Hunters, rescuers, timber workers, Warden-hold families, scouts, ropeway workers, ship-timber crews, or frontier settlers.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Timber contracts, rescue work, convoy protection, family duty, northern expeditions, or disputes over old forest obligations.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Tidefall Atolls</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Aumari homeland of reef flats and shallow-water travel.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Brine pools, tide roads, lagoons, pearl beds, fragile freshwater, and reef routes.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Aumari are dominant; traders and outsiders mostly stay near the main port.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Tide listeners, pearl workers, reef guides, salt workers, stilt-village families, shallow-route pilots, or freshwater guardians.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Navigation work, trade, pilgrimage, reef taboo disputes, storm damage, freshwater crisis, or sea-route obligation.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Hollowdeep</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Axali homeland of grottoes, craft, and controlled water access.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Baths, pigments, textiles, hidden rivers, wells, sinkholes, and hospitality.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Axali are dominant; outsiders are less common away from controlled trade stops.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Well families, textile workers, pigment makers, bath attendants, hospitality houses, water auditors, or hidden-route guides.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Water diplomacy, craft contracts, reservoir disputes, family duty, trade, or questions of access and trust.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Caelmere</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Scolen homeland of civic memory and public works.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Crater lakes, reservoirs, canals, archives, water law, and maintenance.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Scolen are dominant, with traders and workers around canals and lake towns.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Canal workers, lockhouse families, archive assistants, water-law clerks, maintenance crews, engineers, boat workers, or public servants.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Infrastructure work, water disputes, trade service, archival missions, redundancy planning, or civic obligation.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Ashspine</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Kelden homeland of volcanic cliffs and hazard culture.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Ash slopes, vents, ledges, mining, stone judgment, evacuation culture, and dangerous extraction.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Kelden are dominant near habitable coasts.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Stone workers, miners, rescue crews, ash readers, evacuation shrine families, port workers, or hazard specialists.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Extraction contracts, rescue work, warning duty, trade, disaster recovery, or arguments over risk and greed.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Veilgarden</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Thryss homeland of fog valleys and careful stewardship.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Root terraces, damp stone, hidden basins, rare crops, guarded knowledge, and careful agriculture.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Thryss are dominant, with limited merchants near trade points.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Gardeners, herb keepers, memory-keepers, fog-route guides, terrace workers, household apprentices, or people shaped by secrecy.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Rare-crop trade, guarded cures, family duty, disclosure disputes, pilgrimage, or ecological concern.</p>
  </section>
</div>

## Ports, work islands, and frontier origins

<div class="card-grid">
  <section class="card">
    <h2>Breakwater</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Major cosmopolitan harbor, trade hub, repair yard, warehouse island, and outer-route anchor.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Ship repair, warehouses, trade, translation, mixed neighborhoods, and passing crews.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> No single homeland species dominates.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Dock workers, shipwright families, warehouse clerks, customs runners, repair-yard apprentices, merchants, sailors, translators, or mixed port families.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Convoy work, debt, contract labor, trade disputes, smuggling pressure, family business, or because everyone passes through Breakwater eventually.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Rainwall</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Small but vital water-export island.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Storms, cliffs, reservoirs, cisterns, dangerous harbors, and water-loading crews.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Mixed population with water workers, reservoir wardens, harbor callers, and foreign water factors.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Cistern crews, storm workers, reservoir families, ship loaders, water inspectors, or people raised around scarcity and hard weather.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Water contracts, emergency supply work, drought response, repair missions, or political pressure from dependent islands.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Reedmere</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Swampy textile and fiber island.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Reed beds, mangroves, rope, nets, sailcloth, sacks, work cloth, dye yards, and wetland processing.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Mixed population, with strong Axali craft-house influence and northern export interests.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Reed cutters, rope makers, sailcloth workers, dyers, wetland wardens, canal workers, or warehouse families.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Export work, guild disputes, pollution concerns, craft contracts, trade, or overharvest conflict.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Stormhook</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Maritime island of fishing, pilotage, rescue, and dangerous-sea expertise.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Lighthouses, current knowledge, cured fish, harbormasters, and rescue crews.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Mixed maritime population; Aumari, humans, Kelden, Ferren, and fishing communities are especially plausible.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Fishers, pilots, lightkeepers, rescue crews, harbormaster families, cured-fish workers, or people raised around dangerous water.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Convoy pilotage, rescue law, salvage disputes, northern route work, smuggling suspicion, or hidden-route obligations.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Redwind</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Dry range frontier with a major export port.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Salt, herding, wind, caravan roads, water officers, grazing compacts, and port transfer.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Mixed dryland population; Veyr are more common here than on any island outside Sunspire.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Herders, salt workers, caravan guards, water officers, road witnesses, tanners, drying-station workers, or port-transfer families.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Herd drives, salt disputes, drought pressure, road security, trade, or frontier obligation.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Frozenfang</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Harsh seasonal northern mining frontier.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Ferren-led summer camps, mining claims, reused work sites, and expedition logistics.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> No permanent population.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Unusual, but possible for someone raised around seasonal camps, mining claims, expedition logistics, or Greenwood-backed frontier work.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> No one truly stays all year, or a camp, claim, disaster, or vanished expedition still matters.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Verdurain</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Wet hostile jungle frontier.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Two modest permanent camps, dangerous interior, disease, flooding, jungle growth, poor mapping, and failed settlements.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> No island-wide government; origins are tied to camps, river posts, and expeditions.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Unusual origins tied to human or Zivari camps, medicine gathering, botanical study, river posts, or expedition families.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> The interior is hard to govern, a camp failed, a cure is needed, or survival required departure.</p>
  </section>
</div>

## Sacred and danger regions

<div class="card-grid">
  <section class="card">
    <h2>The Veiled Reefs</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Sacred reef region of pilgrimage waters and ritual access.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Taboo lagoons, hidden channels, shrine islets, dangerous routes, and common warnings.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Not a normal island homeland.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Shrine custodians, reef guides, pilgrims, wreck survivors, hermits, or families shaped by pilgrimage.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Pilgrimage, survival, a taboo question, or sacred routes and trade routes colliding.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Western Forbidden Caldera Sea</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Forbidden geographic centerpiece and deadly shortcut.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Volcanic danger, obsidian, toxic waters, ruin rumors, and sacred fear.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Not a normal character origin.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> Relationship or backstory pressure: family loss, sailor warnings, illegal extraction, fear, temptation, or inherited taboo.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Use it as a warning or pressure, not as a place with ordinary homes. Do not reveal secrets.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Cinderreach</h2>
    <p><strong>Identity:</strong> Nearly uninhabitable southeastern desert.</p>
    <p><strong>Known for:</strong> Salt, cinder, heat glare, no true towns, and no normal port.</p>
    <p><strong>Commonly lives there:</strong> Not a normal origin except for rare wreck survivors, exiles, hermits, or failed expeditions.</p>
    <p><strong>Origin use:</strong> A survival scar, rumor, or warning rather than a normal home.</p>
    <p><strong>Why leave:</strong> Survival, exile ending, rescue, or the failure of an expedition.</p>
  </section>
</div>
