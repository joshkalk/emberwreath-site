# Character Creation Guide

Follow these steps when you want a concise path from setting idea to playable character. This guide is conceptual, not mechanical: use your class and background rules normally, then connect them to Emberwreath in a practical way.

## Step 1: Choose species

Species comes first in this campaign's character creation flow. Start with the [Species Comparison]({{ '/species/comparison/' | url }}) if you want a quick decision aid, or browse [Playable Species]({{ '/species/' | url }}) if you want more texture.

Each playable species has a homeland:

- Humans: Breadbasket
- Zivari: Saffra
- Veyr: Sunspire
- Ferren: Greenwood
- Aumari: Tidefall Atolls
- Axali: Hollowdeep
- Scolen: Caelmere
- Kelden: Ashspine
- Thryss: Veilgarden

## Step 2: Choose homeland or adopted island

Your character may come from their species homeland, an adopted island, a trade port, a sea route, a work camp, or a pilgrimage route. Homeland still matters culturally even when your character grew up somewhere else.

Use [Where Are You From?]({{ '/make-a-character/where-are-you-from/' | url }}) if you want help choosing.

## Step 3: Choose culture, tradition, or social world

Pick the community that shaped your character's habits and assumptions. That might be an old homeland tradition, a port household, a convoy crew, a reservoir workforce, a shrine route, a timber camp, or a family still shaped by the eruption 60 years ago.

You do not need to define everything. One clear social world is enough.

## Step 4: Choose class and background

Choose class and background for the character you want to play, then ask how that role fits into Emberwreath's needs. The islands depend on trade in food, water, timber, routes, fiber, medicine, pilots, ports, and other essentials.

Possible concepts include convoy guard, pilgrim, route guide, healer, trader, salvage skeptic, reservoir worker, timber scout, port clerk, or frontier survivor. These are examples, not a complete list.

## Step 5: Choose reason for travel

Your character needs a reason to leave home or stay on the move. It can be ordinary, urgent, reluctant, hopeful, religious, economic, or personal.

Use [Travel Reason Prompts]({{ '/make-a-character/travel-reason-prompts/' | url }}) to choose one or combine two.

## Step 6: Choose one or two setting tensions

Pick one or two tensions your character cares about. Do not try to connect to everything.

- Old homeland traditions versus newer mixed-island life.
- Trade dependence versus local independence.
- Scarcity, caution, and memory after the eruption 60 years ago.
- Sacred routes, dangerous waters, and the reputation of the Veiled Reefs.
- Public duty versus family obligation.
- Curiosity about unresolved mysteries versus respect for warnings and boundaries.

Some mysteries remain intentionally unresolved on the player-facing site. It is fine for your character to have questions without having answers.
