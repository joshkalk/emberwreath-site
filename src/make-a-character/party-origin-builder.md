# Party Origin Builder

Use this page after players have chosen species and rough origins. The goal is to help the DM and players choose a group frame, not to decide the campaign starting location.

<div class="callout">
<strong>Important:</strong> the campaign starting location is undecided. Pick a frame that makes the party easy to bring together, then let the actual start fit the campaign plan.
</div>

## Mostly homeland-connected party

Several characters have strong ties to their species homelands, old traditions, inherited duties, or family expectations.

Use this frame if the group wants culture, obligation, and inter-island comparison to matter from the beginning.

## Mostly outer crescent party

Several characters come from outer islands, edges, routes, frontier work, or communities used to distance from the busiest centers.

If the party leans heavily outer crescent, the campaign may naturally support a roaming, trade-route, frontier, or Firefly-like structure, but this does not lock in the start.

## Mixed port party

The characters know a shared port, dock district, warehouse, shrine stop, convoy office, or busy trade household.

Use this frame if the party wants many species, many obligations, and practical reasons to know each other.

## Trade-route party

The group is tied to movement: food, water, timber, medicine, fiber, pilots, records, passengers, repairs, or contracts.

This frame works well when characters come from different islands but all understand that Emberwreath survives through routes.

## Pilgrimage party

The characters are pilgrims, escorts, shrine workers, skeptics, relatives, guards, guides, or fellow travelers around sacred routes.

The Veiled Reefs can be part of this frame through public belief, practice, warning, and common reputation. Do not treat this as a way to answer hidden mysteries.

## Recovery-work party

The characters are connected by rebuilding, repair, surveys, archives, relocation, rescue, water management, or damage that still traces back to the eruption 60 years ago.

Use this frame if the table wants practical work, memory, scarcity, and institutional caution to matter.

## Frontier-work party

The group shares rough work in places where supply, weather, terrain, and distance matter.

This can include timber scouts, route guards, reservoir workers, port clerks, healers, caravan hands, salvage skeptics, and frontier survivors.

## Ruin-curious party

The characters are interested in old places, damaged records, warnings, taboos, salvage claims, or questions that respectable people may not want handled carelessly.

Keep this frame careful: curiosity does not grant the full truth behind them, and warnings or boundaries can be part of the story without explaining what lies behind them.

## Questions for the table

- What shared place, job, route, vow, or obligation explains why these characters talk to each other?
- Which one or two setting tensions does the party care about most?
- What does the party need from Emberwreath: work, answers, safety, repair, passage, recognition, or distance?
